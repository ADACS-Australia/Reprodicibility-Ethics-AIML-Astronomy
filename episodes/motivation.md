## Motivation: Why this matters for astronomers

### Key points
- ML models are **research outputs**, not just tools
- Non‑reproducible ML results:
  - undermine scientific trust
  - break downstream reuse (catalogues, pipelines)
  - are hard to diagnose after publication
- ML amplifies existing issues in astronomy:
  - large surveys
  - complex pipelines
  - long data lifetimes

### Astronomy‑specific examples (discussion only)
- Source classification models reused across surveys
- Photometric redshift models trained once and reused indefinitely
- Simulation‑trained models applied to real data
