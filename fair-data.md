# 3. Data, models, and FAIRness in ML astronomy (10 min)

### FAIR principles recap (very brief)
- **Findable**
- **Accessible**
- **Interoperable**
- **Reusable**

> Important: FAIR ≠ open  
> FAIR = reusable *by humans and machines*

### Applying FAIR to ML (not just data)
In astronomy ML, FAIR should apply to:
- Training data
- Labels and annotations
- Feature engineering steps
- Trained models
- Derived catalogues

### Where astronomy does well
- Archives and long‑term stewardship
- Community standards (e.g. IVOA)
- Culture of reuse

### Where ML breaks the chain
- Private or undocumented labels
- One‑off preprocessing scripts
- Models shared without training context
- Missing provenance for derived products

### Short activity (optional)
Ask learners to silently identify:
- One thing in their current ML workflow that is **not** FAIR

:::: instructor

No sharing required — this is reflective, not performative.

::::

> Astronomy is already close to FAIR by design — ML is where the cracks show.
